## What are Graph Neural Networks?

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are a class of deep learning models designed to perform inference on data structured as graphs. Unlike traditional neural networks that operate on fixed-size inputs like vectors or grids, GNNs can handle arbitrary graph structures with varying numbers of nodes and edges.

## Key Concepts

### Message Passing

The fundamental operation in most GNNs is message passing, where nodes exchange information with their neighbors. In each layer, a node aggregates messages from its neighbors and updates its representation accordingly.
