\cvsection{Research Summary}

\descriptionstyle{
My research focuses on designing algorithms for graph-structured machine learning problems. Such research is critical for many real-world applications with inherent graph structures, e.g., operations research, chemistry, and natural language processing. The main areas of my works are as follows:\vspace{.15in}
\begin{cvitems}
\item Approximate Inference in Probabilistic Graphical Models (NeurIPS 2015, NeurIPS 2016, NeurIPS 2017, IEEE TIT 2017, AISTATS 2018, ICML 2018),
\item variational inference (NeurIPS 2017),
\item approximate sequential elimination (AISTATS 2018, ICML 2018),
\item graph neural network and reinforcement learning (ICML 2020).
\end{cvitems}
\vspace{.15in}
Recently, I am most interested in using \textbf{deep neural networks} to perform \textbf{structured inference} instead of designing algorithms from scratch. This is attractive since neural networks can learn the underlying structure of the problem from data while relying less on expensive expert knowledge. To highlight, I have successfully developed and applied such ``neural algorithms'' for the following problems:
\vspace{.15in}
\begin{cvitems}
\item solving combinatorial optimization (ICML 2020),
\item automating design of molecules (Arxiv 2020).
\end{cvitems}
\vspace{.15in}
I strongly believe such research directions to advance machine learning research, and have great impact in the real world.
}
